Heritage of Warangal

Warangal Fort

Warangal Fort is a historical fort in Warangal, Telangana, India. It served as the capital of the Kakatiya dynasty during the medieval period.

Thousand Pillar Temple

Thousand Pillar Temple is a historic temple built by the Kakatiya dynasty. It is renowned for its architecture and features numerous intricately carved pillars.

Bhadrakali Temple

Bhadrakali Temple is a significant Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Bhadrakali. It is a popular pilgrimage site in Warangal.

Kakatiya Kala Thoranam

Kakatiya Kala Thoranam is a historical arch in Warangal, representing the architectural grandeur of the Kakatiya dynasty.

Eturnagaram Wildlife Sanctuary

Eturnagaram Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area and a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. It houses diverse flora and fauna.

Ramappa Temple

Ramappa Temple, also known as the Ramalingeswara Temple, is an ancient temple with intricate carvings and remarkable architecture.

Pakhal Lake

Pakhal Lake is a serene reservoir surrounded by hills, providing a picturesque setting for nature lovers.

Rayaparthy Shiva Temple

Rayaparthy Shiva Temple is a historic temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its cultural and religious significance.

Govindarajula Gutta

Govindarajula Gutta is a hillock offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular trekking spot.

Nelakondapalli

Nelakondapalli is an archaeological site with ancient artifacts, including the ruins of a Buddhist stupa and other structures.
